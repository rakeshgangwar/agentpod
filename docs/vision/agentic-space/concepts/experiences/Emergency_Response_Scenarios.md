## âš¡ Crisis Response Architecture

### Emergency Classification System

#### **Level 1: Minor Incidents**
**Localized issues with minimal organizational impact**

**Characteristics**:
- Limited scope affecting single team or function
- Minimal business disruption (< 2 hours)
- No external stakeholder impact
- Standard resources sufficient for resolution

**Response Pattern**:
- Local team and relevant AI agents handle resolution
- Automated documentation and learning capture
- Optional notification to management
- Standard recovery procedures

**Examples**:
- Individual system outage or technical glitch
- Minor data quality issues
- Routine security alert resolution
- Single team workflow disruption

#### **Level 2: Moderate Emergencies**
**Multi-functional issues requiring coordinated response**

**Characteristics**:
- Multiple teams or districts affected
- Business disruption (2-8 hours potential)
- Limited external stakeholder impact
- Cross-functional coordination required

**Response Pattern**:
- Emergency coordination center activation
- Multi-agent crisis team formation
- Management notification and involvement
- Coordinated recovery with stakeholder communication

**Examples**:
- Multi-system integration failures
- Significant security breach containment
- Major client issue requiring cross-functional response
- Supply chain disruption affecting operations

#### **Level 3: Major Crises**
**Organization-wide emergencies requiring full response mobilization**

**Characteristics**:
- Enterprise-wide impact potential
- Significant business disruption (8+ hours)
- Major external stakeholder impact
- Full crisis management team activation

**Response Pattern**:
- Executive crisis team immediate activation
- All-district coordination and resource mobilization
- External stakeholder communication coordination
- Full business continuity plan implementation

**Examples**:
- Comprehensive cybersecurity attack
- Major system infrastructure failure
- Regulatory compliance crisis
- Natural disaster affecting operations

#### **Level 4: Catastrophic Events**
**Existential threats requiring survival response**

**Characteristics**:
- Potential threat to organizational survival
- Extended business disruption (days/weeks)
- Critical stakeholder and public impact
- Maximum resource mobilization required

**Response Pattern**:
- Survival mode operations activation
- Complete leadership team engagement
- Emergency communication to all stakeholders
- Fundamental business model adaptation

**Examples**:
- Complete infrastructure destruction
- Major regulatory/legal existential threat
- Market collapse affecting business viability
- Pandemic-level operational disruption
